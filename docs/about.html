Our Story

In May 2014, Scientific American discovered that children of mothers with a lower education had noisier, weaker and more variable neural activity in response to sound and greater activity in the absence of sound. However, those who learned to play a musical instrument for at least four years exhibited far faster neural responses to speech even after four decades. In 2015, the Washington Post reported that 51% of children fall under the reduced and free lunch qualifications, marking an explosion in the number of underprivileged youth. Despite music being one of the most valuable developmental activities for youth, more than half of them are unable to afford lessons.

Seeking to solve this growing problem, our team designed and implemented Piano++, an innovative, realtime musical experience that allows anyone to instantly connect with music mentors from around the world to learn from the comfort of their own homes. Because our software is compatible with any MIDI keyboard, users of all backgrounds have the chance to flourish and develop their minds.
